01.

Chemical basis of insect adaptation to plant toxin


Human inability to feed unprocessed cassava, poisoning due to extended feeding of grass pea, dermatitis in farmers exposed to congress grass and the recent case of litchi toxicity among the children of Muzaffarpur district in India - these cases remind us what a plant toxin is.

Trying to defend themselves using chemical means, plants restrict digestive capability or target physiological processes of the herbivore species. At the same time, it is hard to imagine a plant species which is not a host of one herbivore species at least.

Here, we are exploring an unexamined adaptation of a specialized insect herbivore (Zygogramma bicolorata) to the phytotoxin of an obnoxious weed plant (Parthenium hysterophorus).

02.

Evolution of the small founding populations


A small founding population is observed when a handful of inbreeding individuals gets dispersed/transported from the parent population and becomes the founding members in establishing a new isolated population.

Founding population is characterized by a reduced standing genetic variation, and is prone to inbreeding depression, demographic stochasticity and genetic drift.

Here, in our research group, we have planned for the genomic scan of the beetle (Zygogramma bicolorata) population in India to understand the role of habitat heterogeneity and local environmental stressors in improving the population-level genomic variability.
